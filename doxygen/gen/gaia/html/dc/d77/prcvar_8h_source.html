00037 /*
00038 ** The thread that waits on a condition is blocked in a "waiting on
00039 ** condition" state until another thread notifies the condition or a
00040 ** caller specified amount of time expires. The lock associated with
00041 ** the condition variable will be released, which must have be held
00042 ** prior to the call to wait.
00043 **
00044 ** Logically a notified thread is moved from the "waiting on condition"
00045 ** state and made "ready." When scheduled, it will attempt to reacquire
00046 ** the lock that it held when wait was called.
00047 **
00048 ** The timeout has two well known values, PR_INTERVAL_NO_TIMEOUT and
00049 ** PR_INTERVAL_NO_WAIT. The former value requires that a condition be
00050 ** notified (or the thread interrupted) before it will resume from the
00051 ** wait. If the timeout has a value of PR_INTERVAL_NO_WAIT, the effect
00052 ** is to release the lock, possibly causing a rescheduling within the
00053 ** runtime, then immediately attempting to reacquire the lock and resume.
00054 **
00055 ** Any other value for timeout will cause the thread to be rescheduled
00056 ** either due to explicit notification or an expired interval. The latter
00057 ** must be determined by treating time as one part of the monitored data
00058 ** being protected by the lock and tested explicitly for an expired
00059 ** interval.
00060 **
00061 ** Returns PR_FAILURE if the caller has not locked the lock associated
00062 ** with the condition variable or the thread was interrupted (PR_Interrupt()).
00063 ** The particular reason can be extracted with PR_GetError().
00064 */
00065 NSPR_API(PRStatus) PR_WaitCondVar(PRCondVar *cvar, PRIntervalTime timeout);

00067 /*
00068 ** Notify ONE thread that is currently waiting on 'cvar'. Which thread is
00069 ** dependent on the implementation of the runtime. Common sense would dictate
00070 ** that all threads waiting on a single condition have identical semantics,
00071 ** therefore which one gets notified is not significant. 
00072 **
00073 ** The calling thead must hold the lock that protects the condition, as
00074 ** well as the invariants that are tightly bound to the condition, when
00075 ** notify is called.
00076 **
00077 ** Returns PR_FAILURE if the caller has not locked the lock associated
00078 ** with the condition variable.
00079 */
00080 NSPR_API(PRStatus) PR_NotifyCondVar(PRCondVar *cvar);

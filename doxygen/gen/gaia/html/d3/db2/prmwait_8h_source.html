00039 /*
00040 ** ENUMERATION: PRMWStatus
00041 ** DESCRIPTION:
00042 **      This enumeration is used to indicate the completion status of
00043 **      a receive wait object. Generally stated, a positive value indicates
00044 **      that the operation is not yet complete. A zero value indicates
00045 **      success (similar to PR_SUCCESS) and any negative value is an
00046 **      indication of failure. The reason for the failure can be retrieved
00047 **      by calling PR_GetError().
00048 **
00049 **  PR_MW_PENDING       The operation is still pending. None of the other
00050 **                      fields of the object are currently valid.
00051 **  PR_MW_SUCCESS       The operation is complete and it was successful.
00052 **  PR_MW_FAILURE       The operation failed. The reason for the failure
00053 **                      can be retrieved by calling PR_GetError().
00054 **  PR_MW_TIMEOUT       The amount of time allowed for by the object's
00055 **                      'timeout' field has expired w/o the operation
00056 **                      otherwise coming to closure.
00057 **  PR_MW_INTERRUPT     The operation was cancelled, either by the client
00058 **                      calling PR_CancelWaitFileDesc() or destroying the
00059 **                      entire wait group (PR_DestroyWaitGroup()).
00060 */

00063     PR_MW_PENDING = 1,
00064     PR_MW_SUCCESS = 0,
00065     PR_MW_FAILURE = -1,

00266 **      to semantically group various wait requests. Each wait group is

00352 /*
00353 ** FUNCTION:    PR_EnumerateWaitGroup
00354 ** DESCRIPTION:
00355 **      PR_EnumerateWaitGroup is a thread safe enumerator over a wait group.
00356 **      Each call to the enumerator must present a valid PRMWaitEnumerator
00357 **      rererence and a pointer to the "previous" element returned from the
00358 **      enumeration process or a NULL.
00359 **
00360 **      An enumeration is started by passing a NULL as the "previous" value.
00361 **      Subsequent calls to the enumerator must pass in the result of the
00362 **      previous call. The enumeration end is signaled by the runtime returning
00363 **      a NULL as the result.
00364 **
00365 **      Modifications to the content of the wait group are allowed during
00366 **      an enumeration. The effect is that the enumeration may have to be
00367 **      "reset" and that may result in duplicates being returned from the
00368 **      enumeration.
00369 **
00370 **      An enumeration may be abandoned at any time. The runtime is not
00371 **      keeping any state, so there are no issues in that regard.
00372 */
00373 NSPR_API(PRRecvWait*) PR_EnumerateWaitGroup(
00374     PRMWaitEnumerator *enumerator, const PRRecvWait *previous);

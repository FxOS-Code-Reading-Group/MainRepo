00018   /*
00019    * W3C specification values: http://dev.w3.org/geo/api/spec-source-orientation.html
00020    *
00021    * Alpha, beta and gamma are Tait-Bryan angles (Z-X'-Y''), that is they correspond to
00022    * yaw, pitch and roll in flight coordinates. More specifically, assume the device
00023    * is resting face up on a flat surface (its screen is pointing straight up). Then
00024    * the X axis points to the right when looking at the screen, the Y axis points up
00025    * when looking at the screen, and the Z axis points toward you when looking at
00026    * the screen. Alpha/beta/gamma then define the device's orientation by applying
00027    * the following rotations to the device in order:
00028    *
00029    * 1) Rotate it by alpha degrees around its Z axis (like a compass, or like changing
00030    *    the yaw of an aircraft, assuming the Y axis is the nose of the craft); alpha
00031    *    is in [0,360).
00032    * 2) Rotate it by beta degrees around its X axis (tilting the top of the device
00033    *    towards or away from you, like pitching an aircraft); beta is in [-180,180).
00034    * 3) Rotate it by gamma degrees around its Y axis (tilting it sideways, like
00035    *    rolling an aircraft); gamma is in [-90,90).
00036    */
